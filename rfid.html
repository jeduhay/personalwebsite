RFID Pathways

About the client

Birch Aquarium at Scripps aims to promote ocean conservation and education by serving as the public outreach center for Scripps Institute of Oceanography at University of California, San Diego. In preparation for Expedition (opening May 2018), they seek to engage visitors in understanding global phenomena at a human scale and human impact on a global scale.

Project brief

From July – September 2017, I worked within a multidisciplinary team to research and design ways to foster interaction and learning at museum exhibits through the use of RFID technology. Under the guidance of EnVision Maker Studio and in collaboration with Birch Aquarium at Scripps, we were responsible for delivering design updates, budget and continuity reports, and a working prototype of an RFID-enabled expeditionary pathway experience. I served as the UI / UX lead of my team and performed the following tasks:

  • Front-end web development (HTML, CSS, JavaScript)
  • Graphic design (Adobe Photoshop, Adobe Illustrator)
  • Rapid prototyping (user testing, wireframing, heuristic evaluation)

Final deliverables

  • Prototype
  • Poster
  • Continuity report
